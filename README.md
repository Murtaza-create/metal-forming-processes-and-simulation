Metal Forming Processes and Nonlinear Simulation
Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive study of metal forming processes, material behavior during plastic deformation, industrial applications, and nonlinear simulation using SolidWorks. It combines manufacturing theory with engineering simulation to understand how different forming processes influence material properties and component performance.

Objectives
Study major metal forming processes.
Understand mechanical property changes during forming.
Analyze industrial applications of metal forming.
Investigate current challenges and emerging technologies.
Perform nonlinear simulation using SolidWorks.
Problem Statement

Modern manufacturing requires lightweight, high-strength components with improved mechanical performance. Selecting the appropriate metal forming process and understanding its influence on material behavior are essential for producing reliable engineering components.

Methodology
Literature review of metal forming processes
Analysis of material deformation mechanisms
Study of work hardening, anisotropy, and residual stresses
Investigation of industrial applications
Review of recent technological innovations
Nonlinear simulation using SolidWorks
